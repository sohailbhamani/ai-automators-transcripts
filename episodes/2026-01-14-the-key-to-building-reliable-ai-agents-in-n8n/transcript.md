# The KEY to Building Reliable AI Agents in n8n

Most AI agents are great for quick short-lived conversations, but if you want them to follow a real business process, such as processing customer applications or handling the return of products or multi-step troubleshooting then you generally need to go a level deeper. The problem is that basic agents can't follow where they are in a business workflow. They don't reliably track what information they've collected, what steps have been completed, and what needs to happen next. This can lead to agents that skip critical steps and lose context between conversations and ultimately deliver unreliable results. This is what state machines solve. Think of it like giving your AI agent access to a checklist that it needs to work through. Your agent can then use this instead of unreliably trying to use its long conversation history to keep track of where it is. We've worked with hundreds of community members building production-grade AI agents. And knowing how to build agents that can reliably manage their state unlocks a huge amount of realworld business use cases. In this video, I'll be showing you two key approaches to managing state within your AI agents. One of which uses a pretty powerful NTN tool that a lot of people seem to have missed. So, make sure to stick around for that. I'll be working through examples like a customer support chatbot as well as a product returns agent that needs to work through multiple steps and then gather and validate that information reliably and then these examples can be used as the basis for far more sophisticated state machine workflows. It's easy to create basic agents within n but if you need them to work reliably across lots of different stages and steps then you should definitely consider building a state machine.
